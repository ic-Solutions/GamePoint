Game Point 4.0 Rules

  1. Tournament will be played across 4 categories:
    • Men’s Single
    • Women’s Single
    • Men’s Doubles
    • Mixed Doubles (Note: Your team can have Only Women or Mixed is up to you)
  2. Tournament will be played in Nylon shuttle.
  3. Tournament will be played in an Indoor stadium over Synthetic or Wooden court.
  4. Non-Markable shoes are mandatory. If not can play bare footed.
  5. Tournament will be based on Knock-Out format.
  6. All Qualifiers will be of 21 points (1 set).
      NOTE:
    1. Every time there is a serve - There is a pointscored.
    2. The side winning the rally adds a point to its score.
    3. At 20 all, the side which gains 2-point lead first, wins the game.
    4. At 29 all, the side scoring 30th point, wins the game.
    5. When the leading score reached 11 points, players have a 60 second interval
  7. Semi-Finals and Finals will be of 21 points (Best of 3 set).
      NOTE:
    1. Every time there is a serve- There is a pointscored.
    2. The side winning the rally adds a point to itsscore.
    3. At 20 all, the side which gains 2-point lead first, wins the game.
    4. At 29 all, the side scoring 30th point, wins the game.
    5. When the leading score reached 11 points, players have a 60 second interval
    6. In third game, players change ends when the leading score reaches 11points
    7. A 2-minute interval between each set is allowed.
  8. Points will be on side over basis.
      Ex: If PlayerA is serving and PlayerB breaks the serve, PlayerB will be rewarded with apoint and service side over will be declared.
  9. State and National level players ranked below 50 are not allowed.
  10. Line referee will be there only for Semi-Finals and Final. Qualifier will have only main referee.
  11. Referee’s decision will be Final decision. In case of any dispute the stand taken by the organizing committee on the matter shall be final.
  12. The organizing committee reserves the right to insert bye laws at any point of time and also reject the entry of any team/individual.
  13. Any indecent act at the venue will be not be entertained , in those cases the participant will be asked to leave the venue then and there.
General Instructions for Singles:
  • At the beginning of the game (0-0) and when the server’s score is even, the server serves from the right service court. When the server’s score is odd, the server serves from the left service court.
  • If the server wins a rally, the server scores a point and then serves again from the alternate service court.
  • If the receiver wins a rally, the receiver scores a point and becomes the new server. They serve from the appropriate service court – left if their score is odd, and right if it is even.

General Instructions for Doubles:
  • A side has only one ‘service’.
  • The service passes consecutively to the players.
  • At the beginning of the game and when the score is even, the server serves from the right service court. When it is odd, the server serves from the left court.
  • If the serving side wins a rally, the serving side scores a point and the same server serves again from the alternate service court.
  • If the receiving side wins a rally, the receiving side scores a point. The receiving side becomes the new serving side.
  • The players do not change their respective service courts until they win a point when their side is serving.
